Reducing Alcohol Consumption With Home Health Care Services

Apr 6, 2017 by Bob Redding

Over time, excessive consumption of alcohol may lead to a myriad of health complications such as liver damage, several types of cancer and even brain damage.What’s more, drinking alcohol excessively can also worsen the symptoms of already existing chronic health conditions in seniors such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and ulcers. Those seniors who frequently drink alcohol are also at a higher risk of heart attack and heart-related diseases. To make matters worse, drinking alcohol can also induce side effect from medication and reduce their efficiency.

By refraining from consuming alcohol, your loved one can improve their health significantly. Home health care services in Reno, NV can encourage your loved one to limit their alcohol consumption to a minimum. To achieve this, your loved one might want to consider introducing certain changes into their lifestyle.

Though this might seem difficult for seniors who resort to alcohol in stressful situations or are used to indulging themselves with a glass of wine or beer during a meal or at social events, a professional caregiver can recommend healthier alternatives.

For example, instead of drinking ordinary wine, your parent can opt for blackberry wine, which is very healthy and contains less volume of alcohol. Seniors who preferred beer can try drinking non-alcoholic ginger ale or root beer instead.

Furthermore, experts from home health care services in Reno, NV can consult with your loved one’s physician and ask for advice on whether your parent can enjoy an occasional glass or two or if it would be best if they stopped drinking completely. If needed, a professional caregiver can drive and accompany your loved one to visit the local hospital for a regular check-up of their liver health and kidney functions.

Seasoned caregivers from home health care services in Reno, NV are trained to notice signs of alcohol abuse and to act accordingly. In order to discourage your loved one’s drinking habits and help prevent cravings, a professional caregiver can also make sure to remove all alcoholic beverages from your parent’s home.

In addition to this, to keep their mind away from drinking, a professional caregiver can recommend that your parent picks up an interesting hobby or maybe adopts a light exercise regimen. This way, they might be able to completely stop drinking while staying active at the same time. This can surely have a positive impact on their overall health.

The truth is that your parent doesn’t need alcohol to stay in a good mood. With the help from home health care services in Reno, NV your loved one can refrain from alcohol, lead a much healthier lifestyle, and stay vital for longer.
